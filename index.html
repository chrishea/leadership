Leadership Cloudcroft is a new initiative designed to address an old problem in Cloudcroft: developing volunteer leaders who are prepared to take charge.

Leadership Cloudcroft aims to help cultivate the next generation of community champions to address our mountain village's unique challenges by identifying and preparing community leaders. 

The Challenge

As a small mountain village, Cloudcroft depends on volunteers from the community to lead its local institutions: the government, school board, library, fire department, and numerous committees that help shape its public services. Despite these volunteers' critical roles, there is no formal effort to identify, recruit, or train these leaders. 

Most take office with no background or experience in their new jobs. That lack of preparation can lead to oversights, mistakes, and missed opportunities.

The Goals

The objective of Leadership Cloudcroft is identify and help prepare community leaders.

Participants will get practical knowledge about the issues facing the Village. Class members gain valuable knowledge and insights into the public issues shaping Cloudcroft's future through monthly sessions featuring expert speakers, immersive site visits, and collaborative projects.

The more they learn, the better they can share their knowledge within the community and take effective action as leaders.

The Program

The Class of 2025 will occur over five months: June through October 2025. There will be six class days, each focusing on a specific topic.

Classes will be held in a centrally-located space in the Village, starting in the morning and running through early afternoon. We anticipate getting everyone out by 3 p.m.

Classes are designed to be highly interactive, allowing participants to discuss issues with the faculty actively. We also anticipate small working groups collaborating on assignments to address particular problems.

The Curriculum

The program focuses on our top challenges: Governance, Infrastructure, Fire, Economic Development, Healthcare, Schools, and more.

We will take a deep dive into these topics. The faculty features people intimately familiar with a topic and offers a first-hand look at the challenges and opportunities. 

In some cases, we visit sites for a first-hand look, such as a tour of the village water/wastewater facilities.
